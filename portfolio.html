Soul Brush

An interactive audiovisual system combining traditional Chinese ink-wash painting aesthetics, Blue-and-White Porcelain colour language, and live music performance. The system uses Processing to generate fluid, ink-like visuals and MaxMSP to capture MIDI performance from GuZheng-inspired sounds. Real-time OSC communication links sound gestures to digital “brush strokes,” allowing sound to paint. The work explores Taoist Yin–Yang ideas and Pomo (泼墨) spontaneity, treating sound as the brush and the screen as living ink.

Circlet

A visual music composition created in Adobe After Effects, inspired by Sun Wukong’s golden circlet from Journey to the West. The piece translates the tension between control and freedom into rhythm, motion, and colour. Red and yellow dominate the palette, reflecting spiritual pressure, rebellion, and inner energy. The work draws on traditions of abstract visual music (McLaren, Fischinger, Kandinsky), using geometric repetition and synchronised timing to connect musical development and visual transformation.

Be Water

A spatial audio composition for 7.1 surround and binaural playback. The piece treats spatialisation itself as a musical parameter: motion, placement, and perspective shifts in the sound field become part of the composition. The work blends first-order and second-order Ambisonics, studio recordings, and electronic material, produced and mixed in Reaper. Conceptually it follows Bruce Lee’s “be water” philosophy – moving between force and softness, structure and fluidity.

The Spirit Land

A first-person brain–computer interface (BCI) game built in Unity that is controlled by the player’s mental state in real time. The system uses non-invasive EEG (OpenBCI Cyton + OpenBCI GUI) to read two different brainwave conditions: focused mental effort (beta activity) and relaxed state. In the game, concentration drives walking (“Beta Walk”), while calmness triggers environmental events such as summoning a fish, releasing a butterfly, or revealing a bridge. The landscape, lighting, and music are designed as a night-time natural world to encourage immersion and to guide the player toward these mental states. The project explores how neural input can become a core interaction mode for immersive environments, and is designed with future VR adaptation in mind.
