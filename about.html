About Me

I am a graduate of M.Phil Music and Media Technology student in Trinity College Dublin, and currently studying my second postgraduate programme in M.Sc Interactive Digital Media also in Trinity College Dublin. My work explores the intersection of sound, modern technology, and experience design on how music, storytelling, and media interaction can create emotional and memorable moments using media technology.

I enjoy taking ideas from concept to completion, whether composing and producing sound, developing interactive media, or designing audiovisual experiences. I also work in the field of developing games for Brain Computer Interface using duo hybrid brain-wave systems.

I’m comfortable bridging artistic vision and technical execution, ensuring creativity meets precision. In both academic projects and community work, I’ve often been the person who connects people, plans structure, and makes sure “the vision actually happens” when it is needed.

Leadership & Student Life

I currently serve as Chairperson of the Trinity College Dublin Chinese Society (CCSA), representing and supporting over 600 Chinese students.

In this leadership role, I have been responsible for organising large-scale cultural, social, and networking events, collaborating with university bodies, and helping build an inclusive community for international Chinese students and students who are interested in Chinese culture.

Leading such a big organisation has strengthened my teamwork, communication, and event management skills, while also allowing me to connect cultures and foster meaningful experiences for others.
